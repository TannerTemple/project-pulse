# Project Pulse — Glossary

**Version 1.0**

---

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
This document defines terms used throughout the planning, design, and development of Project Pulse. It provides a standard vocabulary for engineers and stakeholders to reference, and clarifies complex concepts and abbreviations used across all project documents.

### 1.2 Scope
This glossary applies to the Peer Evaluation Tool project. The terms defined here are used in the Vision and Scope document, the Use Case document, and all other project artifacts.

---

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Senior Design Section
Texas Christian University's Department of Computer Science offers a two-course sequence (COSC 40943: Software Engineering and COSC 40993: Senior Design) where senior students work in teams to solve realistic, client-sponsored problems end-to-end — from definition through deployment.

A **Senior Design Section** denotes a particular offering of this two-course sequence. It commences at the end of August and typically concludes by the end of April of the following year.

**Name format:** `YYYY-YYYY` — e.g., `2023-2024`, `2024-2025`.

---

### 2.2 Senior Design Team
A group of 5–6 senior-level students collaborating on a capstone project. The team works together through the full project lifecycle: concept, design, development, testing, and final presentation.

---

### 2.3 TCU Online
The course management system (LMS) used by TCU. Students upload homework and view grades through it. It does **not** support peer evaluations natively.

---

### 2.4 Academic Year
The period used by educational institutions to measure study, typically spanning from early fall of one year to early summer of the next — approximately nine to ten months.

---

### 2.5 Active Week
A week during which a senior design student is required to submit a Weekly Activity Report and a peer evaluation. Inactive weeks (e.g., winter holiday break) are configured by the admin so students are not required to submit during those periods.

> A week starts on **Monday** and ends on **Sunday**. Monday's date serves as the week identifier.

---

### 2.6 Weekly Activity Report (WAR)
A structured overview of a student's progress, challenges, and next steps for a given week. Each WAR consists of one or more **activities**. For each activity, the student provides:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Activity category | Type of work (e.g., Development, Testing) |
| Planned activity | Short title of the activity |
| Description | Detailed description |
| Planned hours | Hours expected |
| Actual hours | Hours actually spent |
| Status | In Progress / Under Testing / Done |

---

### 2.7 Peer Evaluation
A weekly assessment in which team members rate each other's contributions and teamwork. Results help identify strengths and areas for improvement, and ensure accountability within the team.

#### 2.7.1 Public Comments
Optional comments a student provides when evaluating a teammate. Public comments **are** sent to the evaluatee.

#### 2.7.2 Private Comments
Optional comments a student provides for the instructor only. Private comments are **never** shown to the evaluatee.

---

### 2.8 Rubric
A scoring guide used to evaluate peer performance against predefined criteria. Each criterion has a name, description, and maximum score.

**Default rubric criteria:**

| # | Criterion | Description | Max Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Quality of work | How do you rate the quality of this teammate's work? | 10 |
| 2 | Productivity | How productive is this teammate? | 10 |
| 3 | Initiative | How proactive is this teammate? | 10 |
| 4 | Courtesy | Does this teammate treat others with respect? | 10 |
| 5 | Open-mindedness | How well does this teammate handle criticism of their work? | 10 |
| 6 | Engagement in meetings | How is this teammate's performance during meetings? | 10 |

#### 2.8.1 Criterion
An individual standard within a rubric that evaluates a specific aspect of performance (e.g., technical skill, teamwork, communication).
